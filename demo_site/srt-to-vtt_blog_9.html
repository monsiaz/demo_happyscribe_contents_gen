What challenges do e-learning creators face with subtitle formats?

Every online course, webinar, or tutorial video needs captions for accessibility and engagement. Platforms like Moodle, Coursera, and YouTube have specific requirements. WebVTT is often the standard, while many editors and recording tools default to SRT.

This gap forces you to switch formats. You might worry about:

What best practices ensure accurate and compliant VTT files?

Converting is only part of the task. To create a quality VTT you need to follow a few clearinghouse rules. These guidelines help with readability and platform compliance.

  • Keep each caption under two lines where possible and no longer than 42 characters per line.
  • Include speaker labels in brackets if you have multiple voices, for example [Instructor].
  • Avoid overlapping cues. Each caption block should start after the previous one ends.
  • Use sound descriptions in brackets for key effects, like [music] or [applause].
  • Test RTL languages and special scripts to confirm correct display.

Missing a formatting rule can break your VTT reader or cause captions to skip. Always upload a small sample to your target platform and review before rolling out the full video.

What should you watch out for when converting subtitles?

Even the best converters can introduce issues if you’re not careful. Here are common pitfalls and how to avoid them:

  • Timecode drift – If your SRT file was edited or shortened, the original timecodes may not match your final video. Always verify a random sample of captions after conversion.
  • Encoding errors – Some tools mishandle special characters or non-Latin scripts. Choose a converter that supports UTF-8 and test a few lines with accents or symbols.
  • Missing cues – Converters sometimes drop very short captions. If you rely on quick cues for support transcripts or accessibility, spot-check the start and end of each scene.
  • Styling loss – SRT allows limited styling; WebVTT supports CSS-like tags. Read the tool’s guide on styling if you need colors or position tags in VTT format.

Checklist before you publish:

  • Play your video with the VTT file in your chosen player.
  • Check for orphaned cues (captions that linger after the video ends).
  • Validate the VTT file with an online validator or browser console errors.
  • Ensure your video player references the correct file name and path.

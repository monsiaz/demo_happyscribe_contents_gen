What is the difference between SRT and VTT?

Both SRT and VTT are text-based subtitle formats. They list timecodes and caption text. The main difference lies in the header and the timestamp syntax. VTT files start with a “WEBVTT” header and use a period instead of a comma for milliseconds.

  • SRT timestamp example: 00:01:05,500 --> 00:01:08,000
  • VTT timestamp example: 00:01:05.500 --> 00:01:08.000

By understanding these small tweaks, you can convert srt to vtt with nothing but a text editor. This approach is perfect when you have occasional subtitle jobs or when installing software is not an option.

What best practices improve your subtitle workflow?

Even in a manual conversion, you can focus on efficiency and accuracy. Here are some practical tips:

  • Maintain consistent formatting. Use one blank line between cues.
  • Check for overlapping timecodes that may break playback.
  • Validate your final VTT file using an online checker or by loading it in a test HTML page.
  • Keep a style guide for speaker labels, punctuation, and casing.
  • For multi-language projects, include a language tag in the VTT header like WEBVTT - lang:en.

What pitfalls should you avoid when converting subtitles?

Even a simple task can trip you up if you overlook details. Watch out for these common mistakes:

  • Forgotten “WEBVTT” header – the browser will ignore the file without it.
  • Misplaced blank lines – cues must be separated properly.
  • Lingering commas in timestamps – they break the VTT syntax.
  • Hidden formatting from word processors – stick to plain text editors.
  • Not testing in the final player – fix issues early to avoid delays.

By keeping an eye on these points, you ensure a smooth srt to vtt conversion and save time on troubleshooting later.

How can you turn SRT into WebVTT using a text editor?

For small files or one-off conversions, a text editor is your friend. This manual method is fast and works on Windows, Mac, or Linux. You do not need any special software.

  1. Open the SRT file in Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (Mac).
  2. At the very top, add the line WEBVTT and press Enter twice.
  3. Find all timecode lines and replace commas with periods (for example, change 00:00:05,000 to 00:00:05.000).
  4. Save the file with a .vtt extension instead of .srt.

That is all it takes. Your file now follows the WebVTT specification. You can use HappyScribe’s Subtitle editor to preview how captions will look on your video.

Pitfall: Do not leave hidden carriage returns or odd characters. A quick copy/paste into the editor helps spot stray symbols.

What to watch out for when converting srt to vtt

Even with a reliable tool, there are a few things you want to keep an eye on to avoid last-minute errors:

  • Timecode drift. If your SRT file was edited or re-timed in a second tool, check for slight shifts. HappyScribeâ€™s preview mode helps you catch any drift before download.
  • Cue formatting. WebVTT requires a specific header (WEBVTT), optional metadata blocks, and a blank line before every cue. Make sure the header is present and cues start on new lines.
  • Character limits. Streaming platforms often have max characters per caption row or per second. In the editor, you can split long lines or trim text to meet specs.
  • Multilingual subtitles. If you are handling more than one language, keep each language in separate projects or folders. That way you can avoid mixing up timecodes or text tracks.
  • Styling support. VTT supports basic inline styling (italic, bold, color). If your film requires speaker labels or music cues, add Music or similar tags directly in the editor.

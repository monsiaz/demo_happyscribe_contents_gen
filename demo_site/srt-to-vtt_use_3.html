tips for clear accessible subtitles

Creating a VTT file is only part of compliance. Clear, well formatted captions ensure that viewers with hearing loss or audio challenges get the full message. Follow these tips to get the best results.

  • Keep text concise. Aim for no more than two lines of text per caption. This makes it easier to read and follow along.
  • Include speaker labels. Use names or roles to identify who is speaking, especially in interviews or roundtable discussions.
  • Add sound cues. Mark important sounds like [door opens] or [music playing] so viewers don‚Äôt miss context in the scene.
  • Maintain consistent timing. Each caption should stay on screen long enough to read comfortably. A general rule is one word per tenth of a second.
  • Check for spelling and grammar. Typos can distract or confuse viewers. Use the editor‚Äôs search and replace feature to speed up fixes.

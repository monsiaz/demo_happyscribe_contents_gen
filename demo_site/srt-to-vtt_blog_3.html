Can you convert SRT to VTT directly in Premiere Pro

Premiere Pro offers a built-in caption import and export feature. Recent versions let you choose between SRT and VTT when you export sidecar files. So yes, you can convert srt to vtt without leaving your project.

Here is what happens when you import SRT into Premiere Pro:

  • For CC tracks you go to File > Import and select the SRT file.
  • The captions appear in your timeline as a caption track.
  • When you export, you enable “Export captions” and pick “WebVTT (VTT).”

Keep in mind that older versions of Premiere Pro may only export SRT. If you do not see the VTT option, update to the latest release. Or use one of the alternative methods covered later.

Step by step guide to convert srt to vtt

Follow these plain-English steps to import your SRT file, tidy up captions, and export a VTT file:

  1. Open your project and go to File > Import. Select your .srt subtitle file.
  2. In your timeline panel, right-click on the SRT file and choose Create New Caption Track. Pick the right language and style preset.
  3. Drag the caption track onto your video timeline. Align the track above your main video layer.
  4. Click on the caption segment in the timeline to open the Captions panel. Here you can adjust text, timing, and reading speed.
  5. Use the Subtitle editor in Premiere Pro to fix any overlapping captions or missing cues. Keeping your captions under 42 characters per line helps readability.
  6. When you are ready, go to File > Export > Media. In the Export Settings, open the Captions tab.
  7. Select “Create Sidecar File” and pick WebVTT (.vtt) as the format. Set the include option to “Burn Captions into Video” only if you need a hard-coded version.
  8. Click Export. Premiere Pro will generate a .vtt file alongside your video or in your chosen output folder.

Tip Avoid leaving extra spaces at the end of lines in your SRT. Those can cause errors in the VTT file interpretation. A quick scan in the Subtitle editor panel solves most small mistakes.

Best practices for caption editing in Premiere Pro

Once you know how to convert srt to vtt, you can streamline your caption workflow. Here are a few best practices to try:

  • Use Automatic transcription from HappyScribe to generate a rough transcript. This gives you a head start before tweaking in Premiere Pro.
  • Keep each caption cue under two lines. WebVTT supports additional styling but short lines are easier to read.
  • Run a spelling and grammar check on your text. A clear caption build strengthens your brand voice.
  • Match caption timing to speaker pauses. Amplify accessibility by ensuring viewers have time to read between cuts.
  • Save presets in Premiere Pro for caption style, font, and position. This helps when you batch export multiple sequences.

Pitfall Avoid stacking captions at the same timecode. That can confuse players and lead to dropped cues when you convert srt to vtt.

Example Adding “WEBVTT” at the top of an SRT makes a valid VTT if your timing lines are already in format. Just remove commas in timecodes and replace with periods.

why srt to vtt matters for accessibility consultants

As an accessibility consultant, you know that accurate captions are a key part of meeting legal and ethical standards for digital video. Many clients come to you with subtitle files in SRT format that work fine in some players but break on modern web platforms that require VTT. You need a simple solution to convert these files without messing up time codes or styling cues.

Imagine a university client hosting lecture recordings on their learning management system. They have SRT files generated by a transcription vendor. When students try to watch videos, captions do not appear or display incorrectly. You face a critical challenge:

  • The LMS only supports WebVTT (.vtt) captions.
  • Recreating transcripts from scratch costs time and money.
  • Manual conversion risks errors in timing or formatting.

That is where a reliable SRT to VTT conversion workflow comes in. It saves you hours of manual work and helps you deliver an accessible experience that meets WCAG 2.1 standards.

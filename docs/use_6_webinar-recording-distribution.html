Webinar recording distribution: srt to vtt made simple

In this guide, you’ll learn how to convert your webinar captions from SRT to VTT so you can reach more viewers on any platform. We’ll walk you through a real-world scenario, show you each step in the workflow, highlight key HappyScribe features, answer common questions, and end with a quick path to get started today.

Why convert SRT to VTT for webinars?

Imagine you just wrapped up a product launch webinar. You captured every question from your audience and created an SRT file for captions. Now you want to share that recording on your website, YouTube, and an LMS that only accepts VTT. Manually retyping or using a basic converter can be slow, produce errors, and leave viewers frustrated by mismatched captions. By switching to a streamlined SRT to VTT process, you’ll:

  • Keep captions in sync across all platforms
  • Ensure compatibility with major video players
  • Save hours of manual editing and quality checks

Who benefits from this conversion?

  • Marketing teams sharing webinars on YouTube and social media
  • Training departments uploading lessons to an LMS
  • Event producers distributing recorded panels
  • Education teams building on-demand courses

How to set up your SRT to VTT workflow

Follow these steps to automate and speed up your caption conversion using HappyScribe.

  1. Prepare your SRT file
    Export or download the SRT file from your video platform or caption editor. Make sure it’s correctly timed and includes speaker names if needed.
  2. Sign up or log in to HappyScribe
    If you don’t already have an account, create one at happyscribe.com. You’ll get access to AI tools, a subtitle editor, and API keys.
  3. Upload or send your SRT
    You have two options:
    • Web app: Click the “Upload file” button, select SRT, then choose VTT as output.
    • API: Use this call to start a conversion job: 
      POST https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles
Headers:
  Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY
Body:
  {
    "file_url": "https://yourserver.com/yourfile.srt",
    "source_language": "en",
    "output_format": "vtt"
  }
  4. Check the job status
    If you used the API, poll the status endpoint: 
    GET https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles/{job_id}
    Once it’s “complete,” your VTT is ready to download.
  5. Edit with the subtitle editor
    Open the file in HappyScribe’s online editor to adjust timing, correct typos, or add styling for captions. It updates in real time.
  6. Download your VTT
    Click “Download” in the web app, or fetch it via API: 
    GET https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles/{job_id}/download?format=vtt
  7. Publish and share
    Use your VTT file:
    • Embed in your HTML5 player:
      • <video controls>
  <source src="webinar.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  <track kind="captions" src="subtitles.vtt" srclang="en" label="English">
</video>
    • Upload to YouTube or Vimeo for auto-sync
    • Attach to your LMS course for on-demand access

Best practices for webinar subtitle quality

  • Keep each caption under 42 characters to improve readability
  • Break at natural speech pauses, not mid-sentence
  • Review speaker labels and add sound cues (e.g. [music], [applause])
  • Use the editor’s search feature to fix repeated typos in seconds

Tips for distributing your webinars globally

  • Use AI notetaker to pull key points, then share those highlights in multiple languages
  • Leverage translation export to create subtitles in Spanish, French, German and more
  • Set up automatic jobs via API for every new webinar recording
  • Monitor caption accuracy with version history and change logs

News broadcaster use case: convert SRT to VTT with HappyScribe

Imagine you’re the lead editor at a 24/7 news network. Your team produces dozens of video clips every day – breaking news updates, in-depth interviews, and live streams. You use SRT files for internal review and quick edits, but your web and streaming platforms demand VTT files for proper caption display and accessibility. Manual conversion eats up hours, slows down your release schedule, and leaves room for errors. You need a faster, more reliable way to turn SRT into VTT so your audience gets accurate captions on time, every time.

what challenges do news broadcasters face with subtitle conversion?

  • Time pressure: You work under tight deadlines. Every second counts from breaking news to daily segments.
  • Multiple platforms: Web players, social media, live streaming tools – each may require VTT files with specific metadata.
  • Accuracy demands: Caption errors can harm viewer trust, trigger compliance issues, or lead to misunderstandings in fast-moving stories.
  • Team collaboration: Editors, producers, and legal reviewers all need access to the same up-to-date captions.
  • Scalability: As your network grows, so does the volume of subtitle files you must convert and manage.

how can you convert SRT to VTT in five simple steps?

Let’s walk through a clear, repeatable process you can set up in minutes with HappyScribe. You’ll learn how to work in the web app, tap into our API, and streamline the entire flow.

  1. Create or sign in to your HappyScribe account

    Head over to HappyScribe.com and sign up or log in. You’ll land on your dashboard, where you can manage all your transcription and subtitle tasks in one place.

  2. Upload your SRT file

    Click “Upload” and select your SRT file. If you have multiple files, you can upload them in bulk. Within seconds, they appear in your project list ready for action.

  3. Choose VTT as your export format

    In the export options, pick “WebVTT (VTT).” You can adjust settings like caption style, timestamp format, and line length to match your brand guidelines or platform specs.

  4. Convert with a single API call

    If you want full automation, integrate HappyScribe’s API into your content pipeline. Here’s a quick cURL example:

    curl -X POST "https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles" \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "file_url": "https://yourserver.com/path/to/news_clip.srt",
    "output_format": "vtt"
}'

    Or, in Node.js:

    const fetch = require('node-fetch');
    
async function convertSrtToVtt() {
  const response = await fetch('https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles', {
    method: 'POST',
    headers: {
      'Authorization': 'Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN',
      'Content-Type': 'application/json'
    },
    body: JSON.stringify({
      file_url: 'https://yourserver.com/path/to/news_clip.srt',
      output_format: 'vtt'
    })
  });
  const data = await response.json();
  console.log('Conversion job started', data.id);
}

convertSrtToVtt();

    You’ll get back a job ID. Poll the status endpoint until it’s “done,” then fetch your VTT file link. Save or push it to your CMS or streaming tool.

  5. Review and download your VTT

    Back in the HappyScribe app you can:

    • Open the subtitle editor to spot-check timing and text.
    • Use the search bar to jump to specific segments.
    • Download the final VTT file or push it directly to your publishing platform via FTP, S3, or your CMS.

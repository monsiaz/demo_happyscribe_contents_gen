what are SRT and VTT files and why they matter

SRT and VTT are plain text formats that store caption data. Each file lists timed text that appears alongside your video. Here is a quick breakdown:

  • SRT (SubRip text) is a common format for offline editing and legacy tools. It uses a simple timestamp syntax.
  • VTT (WebVTT) is a standard for HTML5 video players and modern web platforms. It adds styling options and WebVTT cues.

For digital marketing purposes, VTT has clear advantages:

  • Native support in most HTML5 players, ensuring captions display properly across browsers.
  • Better styling control for fonts, colors, and positioning‚Äîaligning with your brand identity.
  • Compatibility with ad platforms and social media networks that require VTT for captions.

By converting SRT to VTT, you keep your captions in sync with platform requirements and maintain a consistent look for your campaigns.

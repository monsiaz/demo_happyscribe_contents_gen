why convert SRT to VTT?

  • Platform compatibility: Many online video players and streaming services use VTT. Converting ensures captions display correctly on sites like YouTube, Vimeo, and custom players.
  • Improved styling: VTT lets you add cues, styling tags, and positioning notes that look great on any screen.
  • Accessibility: Accurate captions keep your content accessible to viewers who are deaf or hard of hearing. VTT can include metadata for audio descriptions and chapter markers.
  • Client requirements: Many clients or broadcast outlets ask specifically for VTT. Meeting their format rules keeps projects on track and budgets on pace.

how can video editors convert SRT to VTT without coding?

You don’t need to write a single line of code. With HappyScribe’s online tools, you’ll upload an SRT file, tweak the text or timing if you need to, and export a VTT that’s ready for your final export. It takes just a few clicks, and you stay in control every step of the way.

step 2: upload your SRT file

Once you’re inside the dashboard:

  1. Select Upload file.

step 3: choose VTT as your output format

When processing is complete, you’ll see options for transcript or subtitle editing. To convert SRT to VTT:

  • Click Export at the top right of the editor screen.
  • In the dropdown, select VTT as the file type.
  • Click Download.

Your WebVTT file is on your computer, ready for upload to your video player.

step 4: edit or refine with the subtitle editor

If you need to tweak timing, correct typos, or adjust line breaks before export, HappyScribe’s subtitle editor has you covered:

  • Preview changes by hitting Play inside the editor. Everything updates in real time.

step 5: download and integrate into your video project

After you export your VTT file:

  • Open your video editing software or content management system.
  • Import the VTT file as a caption track.
  • Confirm that captions appear correctly in your preview or live stream player.

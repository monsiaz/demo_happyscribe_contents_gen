How can you convert SRT to VTT with HappyScribe?

Ready to see how it works? We’ll walk you through a step-by-step path, from signing up to pulling your finished VTT files. You can follow our web interface or add this to your own platform using our API.

1. Sign up and upload your SRT

  • Create a HappyScribe account or log in at happyscribe.com.
  • Select “Upload” and choose your SRT file.
  • Give your project a name, like “Q1 product training.”

2. Choose VTT as your target format

  • In the “Export” menu, pick “WebVTT (.vtt)”.
  • Optionally adjust style settings or font size in the subtitle editor.
  • Click “Export” to kick off the conversion.

3. Download or access the VTT file

  • Your VTT file will appear in your project dashboard.
  • Click “Download” or get a shareable URL.
  • Plug into your LMS or video player and confirm captions display correctly.

4. (Optional) Automate with our API

If you handle hundreds of modules each quarter, skip manual steps with a few lines of code:

curl -X POST https://api.happyscribe.com/v1/subtitles/convert \
  -H "Authorization: Token YOUR_API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "source": "https://your-storage.com/corp-training.srt",
    "from_format": "srt",
    "to_format": "vtt",
    "callback_url": "https://yourapp.com/webhook"
  }'

HappyScribe will send a webhook when your VTT is ready. You can then pull it down and feed it into your LMS automatically. Add error checks or logging to track every conversion at scale.

How to get the most from your converted subtitles

Once you’ve got your VTT files in hand, here are tips to make the final push:

  • Match your LMS style: Adjust CSS in your player or LMS to use corporate fonts and colors. VTT supports styling tags for font weight, color, and position.
  • Test on multiple devices: Check desktop, tablet, and mobile views. Some players wrap long lines differently.
  • Run a quick review: Play through a few key sections to confirm captions sync and read clearly. Look for split lines or overlapping time codes.
  • Archive your originals: Keep both SRT and VTT files in your asset library. That way you can re-export with new settings as needed.

With these checks, you’ll deliver a polished learning experience every time.

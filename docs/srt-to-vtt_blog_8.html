What are best practices and pitfalls to avoid when converting and translating subtitles

Even the smoothest workflow can run into hiccups if you’re not careful. Here are some tips to keep your subtitles crisp and reader-friendly:

  • Keep line length under 42 characters: Short lines are easier to read at a glance.
  • Keep sentences digestible: Break at natural pauses to avoid reading overload.
  • Check cultural context: A literal translation may not always convey the right tone or idiom.
  • Maintain speaker labels: Use names or initials so viewers can follow who is talking.
  • Test in your player: Not all video players render VTT the same. Always preview.

Pitfall to avoid: Don’t let automatic translation go unreviewed. Machine translation can misinterpret slang, proper names, or technical terms.

How do I prepare srt files for a smooth workflow?

Before importing your SRT into Premiere, check that your file is clean. A few quick checks help avoid errors later:

  • Open the SRT in a plain text editor. Confirm each caption block has a number, a time range, and text lines.
  • Ensure timecodes follow the format hours:minutes:seconds,milliseconds. For example 00:01:15,300.
  • Remove any blank lines at the start or end of the file.
  • Watch out for special characters that might break the import. Replace accented letters if needed.

What steps do I follow to export vtt from Premiere?

Once your SRT is ready, follow this plain-English checklist to bring it into Premiere and export as VTT:

  1. Launch Premiere Pro and open your project timeline.
  2. Go to the “Captions and Graphics” workspace from the top menu.
  3. Right-click in the Project panel. Choose Import and select your cleaned SRT file.
  4. Drag the imported captions onto a track above your video layer. Align it to match your sequence.
  5. Play through the timeline. Spot-check a few caption entries to confirm proper timing and line breaks.
  6. When you are happy, go to File > Export > Media.
  7. In the Export Settings dialog, set Format to WebVTT.
  8. Under Captions, check Include Captions and confirm WebVTT is selected.
  9. Click Export. Premiere writes out a .vtt file to your chosen folder.

What common pitfalls should I watch out for when working with subtitles?

Even simple tasks have traps. Here are a few missteps to avoid:

  • Avoid overlapping captions. Premiere shows warnings if two captions share the same timeframe.
  • Don’t forget to review punctuation. Poor grammar or missing commas can affect readability.
  • Watch for line length. WebVTT readers may cut off long lines on smaller screens.
  • Verify encoding. Make sure your VTT is saved in UTF-8 to support special characters.
  • Check platform requirements. Some hosts need cues defined explicitly with identifiers.
